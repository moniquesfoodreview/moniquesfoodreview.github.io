Winter Moon

Snow lines each branch
of the tall maple by the barn.
Soon, the waning moon will rise behind it,
Then above it,
As this world to the next.

Dark

Earth and space are less distinct at dusk.
Both are shades of gray.
The fuzziness of all vision is heightened,
And trees seem to combine and divide in the background.
Then they come - the essence of duality:
Impossibly large
And yet so small.
Cutting though the visual morasse
Like God, a shepard,
Calling his flock from prestigious ends.
And to think it occurs distinct,
In the number of starts each night.
A net of jewels too pure for any other world.

Gentle Rain

Cool fall evening.
Floating down from high above

Impossible Question

A robin glides through the forest.
How does she avoid all the trees?
